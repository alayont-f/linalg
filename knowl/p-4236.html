Part (d) shows that neither vector \(\vu_1 = \proj_{\vw_1} \vv\) nor \(\vu_2 = \proj_{\vw_2} \vv\) is the vector in \(W\) that is closest to \(\vv\text{.}\) We should probably expect this since neither projection uses the fact that the other vector might contribute to the closest vector. Let us instead consider the sum \(\vu_3 = \vu_1+\vu_2\text{.}\) Calculate the components of this vector \(\vu_3\) and determine the distance between \(\vu_3\) and \(\vv\text{.}\) Which of the three vectors \(\vu_1\text{,}\) \(\vu_2\text{,}\) and \(\vu_3\) in \(W\) is closest to \(\vv\text{?}\)
